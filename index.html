HEY! HOW'S IT GOING?: Let's get to know
each other (Fillers + Indirect questions)
Preview
Download
This multi-part lesson focuses on improving fluency and polite communication through the use of conversation fillers and indirect questions.
    Key areas include:
  • Fillers for natural speech (e.g., frankly speaking, honestly, on top of that, basically, long story short, etc.)
  • Grammar focus: forming and using indirect questions for clarity and politeness (e.g., Could you tell me where the nearest metro station is? / Do you know if the hotel serves breakfast? / I was wondering what time the meeting starts.)
  • Practice activities: personalized discussions, role-plays, vocabulary and grammar drills
  • Homework and follow-up: additional video exposure and grammar tasks to reinforce indirect questions, vocabulary list
The class can be delivered in one or two sessions, with the first focusing on conversation fillers and discussions, and the second on indirect question grammar. Teachers are encouraged to adapt based on student needs, with flexibility to extend into additional sessions for more practice if needed.

Routines & Rhythms: Building habits
(Habits + USED TO Constructions)
Preview
Download
This multi-part lesson focuses on language for talking about habits and routines, as well as mastering the grammar of "used to," "be used to," and "get used to" constructions.
    Key areas include:
  • Vocabulary for habits and routines (e.g., stick to, once in a while, cut back on, develop a habit, ditch, consistently, etc.)
  • Grammar focus: explaining and applying the differences between “used to”, “be used to”, and “get used to”
  • Practice activities: personalized discussions, sentence rewrites, habit reflection tasks, and video-based speaking prompts
  • Homework and follow-up: quizlet set, grammar and vocabulary reinforcement, additional article and video exposure, and an optional writing task
The class can be split into up to three sessions for deeper practice, starting with vocabulary and habit discussion, followed by grammar instruction, and finishing with extended practice. Teachers are encouraged to adapt based on student needs, with flexibility to extend into additional sessions for more practice if needed.

WHAT WAS IT LIKE? (Memories +
USED TO/WOULD Constructions)
Preview
Download
This multi-part lesson focuses on reflecting on memories and developing advanced fluency in talking about the past, with a strong focus on the grammar of "used to" and "would" for past habits and repeated actions.
    Key areas include:
  • Vocabulary related to memory and remembering (e.g., take a trip down memory lane, pop into one’s mind, everything is just a blur, in hindsight, my mind goes blank when..., etc.)
  • Grammar focus: contrasting and using “used to” vs. “would”
  • Practice activities: storytelling, sentence transformations, vocabulary and grammar drills, and video discussions
  • Homework and follow-up: quizlet set, grammar and vocabulary reinforcement, additional article and video exposure, and an optional writing task
The class can be divided into three sessions for maximum retention. Begin with vocabulary discussion, move on to the video, followed by focused grammar work. Teachers are encouraged to adapt based on student needs, with flexibility to extend into additional sessions for more practice if needed.

MONEY TALK: Prices, inflation, recession...
(Money + Intensifiers in comparison)
Preview
Download
This multi-part lesson focuses on language for discussing prices, inflation, and economic trends, alongside a secondary focus on intensifiers in comparisons for added expressiveness.
    Key areas include:
  • Vocabulary for prices and economics (e.g., inflation, cost of living, tighten one’s belt, skyrocket, plummet, supply and demand, recession, go bankrupt, keep up with prices, etc.)
  • Grammar focus: exploration of intensifiers in comparisons (e.g., way higher, much worse, far too expensive) as a secondary objective to enhance discussion
  • Practice activities: personalized discussions, reading and analyzing an article, listening activity, grammar drill, and reflection on economic strategies and personal experiences
  • Homework and follow-up: quizlet set, vocabulary and grammar reinforcement, additional video exposure, and an optional writing task
The class can be split into up to three sessions for deeper practice, starting with vocabulary introduction and discussion, followed by grammar and listening activity, and finishing with extended speaking and article discussions. Teachers are encouraged to adapt based on student needs, with flexibility to extend into additional sessions for more practice if needed.
